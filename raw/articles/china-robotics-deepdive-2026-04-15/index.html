CONFIDENTIAL | COMPANY APPENDIX | MAY 16–23, 2026 COMPANY FIELD GUIDE | TENTATIVE AGENDA COMPANY FIELD GUIDE Beijing + Shanghai + Hangzhou Frontier embodied-AI names, scale-up humanoids, and the current “robot brain” layer. Beijing builders Embodied AI, robot brains, and next-wave humanoid Galbot (Embodied AI / warehouse robotics — picking, sorting, retail automation) Founded 2023. Builds embodied robots for retail, logistics, and factory workflows. Founder He Wang is a Peking University professor with Tsinghua and Stanford background. Backers include CATL, Meituan, Qiming, GGV, and other major funds. Galaxea AI (Embodied AI / mobile manipulation — robot arms, wheel-based humanoids) Founded 2023. Full-stack embodied-AI company spanning models, data collection, and robot hardware. Co-founded by Xu Huazhe, a Stanford-trained Tsinghua researcher. Backers include Ant Group, Meituan, IDG Capital, and Baidu Ventures. Noetix Robotics (Humanoids — affordable agile robots, bionic platforms) Founded 2023. Beijing startup pushing low-cost, agile humanoids and bionic robots. Founder Jiang Zheyuan left a Tsinghua PhD track to build the company. Financing has included Beijing Robot Fund, Fangguang Capital, and CICC Capital. Qianjue Technology (Robot brains — planning models, embodied AI) Founded 2023. Builds a “brain” layer for robots: decision, planning, and generalized control models across different hardware forms. Founder Gao Haichuan holds a Tsinghua PhD in automation. Investors include Innoangel, Vertex Ventures, Detong, and other Tsinghua-linked / industry funds. Shanghai Scale-up humanoids and full-stack embodied-AI builders AGIBOT (Humanoids — general-purpose robots, data engines) Founded 2023. One of China’s flagship humanoid companies, building general-purpose robots for industrial and service settings. Founded by ex-Huawei leaders Deng Taihua and Peng Zhihui. Investors include LG Electronics, Mirae Asset, and earlier strategic backers reportedly including BYD and HongShan. TARS (Embodied AI stack — data engine, models, full-stack robots) Founded 2025. New embodied-AI company building the full stack: data engine, perception / reasoning models, and robot systems. Led by Chen Yilun (ex-Huawei autonomous-driving CTO, Tsinghua AIR) alongside Baidu autonomous-driving veteran Li Zhenyu. Angel backers include Lanchi, Qiming, Linear, Lenovo Capital, and Hillhouse Venture. Hangzhou Legged-robot scale and one of China’s clearest robotics success stories Unitree / Yushu (Legged + humanoids — quadrupeds, low-cost humanoids) Founded 2016. China’s best-known legged robot company, now also pushing humanoids. Founder Wang Xingxing built the original XDog project before founding Unitree after a short stint in Shenzhen. Public investors have included HongShan, Matrix Partners, and Shunwei. What to notice in these cities • Beijing tends to lead in embodied-AI algorithms, model research, and founder density around Tsinghua / PKU. • Shanghai skews more toward industrialization, scaling, and founder teams with big-company operating history. • Hangzhou shows what a winner looks like once a robot company reaches product velocity and public visibility. CONFIDENTIAL | COMPANY APPENDIX | MAY 16–23, 2026 SHENZHEN HARDWARE STACK Shenzhen + Dongguan + Huizhou Prototyping, 3D vision, autonomy, robot joints, tactile sensing, and the motion-control layer. Shenzhen / Dongguan Builder layer — prototyping, robot OEMs, perception, and autonomy Seeed Studio (Prototyping & manufacturing — open hardware, edge AI / IoT) Founded 2008. Developer-to-factory platform for prototypes, modules, and small-batch manufacturing. Founded by Eric Pan, a former Intel engineer and maker-community builder. Backers have included IDG Capital and Shenzhen Capital Group. LimX Dynamics (Embodied AI / humanoids — bipeds, full-size humanoids) Founded 2022. Builds biped, humanoid, and embodied-AI platforms. Founder Zhang Wei is a SUSTech professor and former Ohio State professor. Investors include Alibaba, JD.com, NIO Capital, Lenovo Capital, and SAIC-linked Shangqi Capital. Orbbec (3D vision — depth cameras, robot perception) Founded 2013. Supplies 3D vision sensors that function as robot “eyes.” Founded by Howard Huang, with Peking University and MIT SMART background. A major disclosed backer has been Ant Financial. AGILEX Robotics (Mobile robots — UGV chassis, autonomy kits) Founded 2016. Builds mobile robot bases used in research, logistics, and autonomy. Founder Jidong Wei previously helped build DJI RoboMaster. Investors include Sequoia China, 5Y Capital, Vertex Ventures China, and HKX. Whale Dynamic (Autonomous driving — last-mile delivery vehicles) Founded 2018. Builds L4 autonomous delivery vehicles and full-stack AV software. Founder David Chang is a Baidu autonomous-driving veteran. Early financing included Qianchuang Capital. Shenzhen Control layer — joints, sensing, motors, tactile intelligence ZeroErr (Robot joints — rotary actuators, encoders) Founded 2016. Makes high-precision robot joints, actuators, and magnetic encoders. Public founder details are light; legal representative / executive is Jia Xiqing. Investors include Bilin Capital, Ambrum Capital, Songbai Investment, Huakong Fund, and Chuangdongfang. XJCSENSOR (Force sensing — torque, six-axis sensors) Founded 2009. Builds force, torque, pressure, and six-axis sensors used in robotics and automation. Led by chairman Wu Hao; 2025 outside financing was led by Shenzhen Capital Group with Juhe Capital and Renzhi Capital. Damiao Technology (Actuation — joint motors, wheel hubs) Founded 2019. Makes motors and drive systems used in quadrupeds, robot arms, and humanoids. Founded by former DJI engineers. Public financing disclosure is limited. Daimon Robotics (Tactile robotics — dexterous hands, VTLA models) Founded 2023. Focused on tactile sensing, dexterous manipulation, and vision-tactile-language-action models. Originated from HKUST research; co-founded by Wang Yu and Duan Jianghua. Backers include Lenovo Capital, Jinding, China Merchants Venture, and China Mobile-linked capital. ZHAOWEI (Precision components — micro-drives, dexterous hands) Founded 2001. Precision micro-drive supplier increasingly relevant to humanoids and dexterous-hand systems. Its CES 2025 dexterous-hand launch showed how mature component makers are moving up the stack. Lantern x the Mu — China Frontier Immersion — Appendix: Shenzhen Hardware Stack I

CONFIDENTIAL | COMPANY APPENDIX | MAY 16–23, 2026 SHENZHEN HARDWARE STACK Manufacturing & scale layer The companies that turn iteration into shipped product: desktop hardware, OEM scale, servo systems, and EMS execution. Shenzhen / Dongguan Manufacturing, systems integration, and execution depth Bambu Lab (3D printing — desktop printers, hardware execution) Founded 2020. Not a robot OEM, but one of the clearest examples of Shenzhen productization speed. Founded by Tao Ye and a heavily ex-DJI team. Publicly linked to IDG Capital. Topband (OEM manufacturing — intelligent controls, motors) Founded 1996. Large intelligent-control and manufacturing platform spanning appliances, energy, vehicles, and robotics. Led by founder-chairman Wu Yongqiang. A public company, so the key takeaway here is scale, systems integration, and factory depth. Morikawa Motor (Servo systems — high-end servo motors) Founded 2022. Focused on high-end servo motor systems and related drives. Best understood as a motion-control supplier rather than a branded robot company. Public founder and investor details are limited. Jiewill Electronics (EMS / ODM — PCBA, contract manufacturing) Established manufacturing-services provider offering PCB design, assembly, sourcing, and box build. Best viewed as an execution and contract-manufacturing stop. Public founder and investor details are limited. What participants should pay attention to on the ground • How quickly teams move from prototype to small-batch manufacture. • Which companies are selling a complete robot versus a critical subsystem. • Where the bottlenecks really sit: data, dexterity, cost-down, yield, or supply-chain coordination. • How founder pedigree translates — or does not translate — into product and customer traction. A practical way to read the trip • Beijing / Shanghai / Hangzhou show where frontier robot companies are trying to go. • Shenzhen shows what it takes to actually build the stack: sensors, joints, motors, factories, and iteration loops. • Together, the cities tell a fuller story than either the software or hardware layer alone.
